Top signals from this view findings that survive without hour-of-day data

🇮🇪 Ireland — inverted weekend pattern

~EUR 13.5k spent Mon–Fri produced a -EUR 1,629 loss. Same period spent ~EUR 1k Sat–Sun and produced +EUR 695 profit.

Saturday APR+ is 58% on tiny spend. Suggests either (a) hard weekday pulls, or (b) aggressive Saturday push — opposite of the standard "weekend wind-down" rule shape.

🇿🇦 South Africa — Saturday is loss-making at volume

~ZAR 42k weekly Saturday spend at -8.4% APR+ burns ~ZAR 3.5k/wk = ~ZAR 180k/yr saved if pulled to ×0.20 (similar to UK/US evening rule). Sunday is marginal but positive.

🇩🇪 Deutschland — Saturday at -40% APR+

Small spend (~EUR 830/wk Sat), but the worst-performing day-of-week of any market we measured. Hard Saturday pull is a no-brainer once you're building the ruleset.

🇳🇿 New Zealand — weekends are MORE profitable, not less

Sat 59% and Sun 60% APR+ vs Mon 48%. NZ wants a weekend push rule, not a pull. Inverts the UK/US baseline. Note: very low spend overall (~NZD 28/day) — volume needs to come up before this matters in absolute £.

🇨🇦 Canada — boring profitable, ride steady

Every day 27–44% APR+. Largest absolute market alongside ZA. A small Sun trim (~×0.85) and otherwise no daypart aggression needed.

🇦🇺 Australia & 🇫🇷 France — too thin to design rules

AU is 4.6k AUD over 90d (~50 AUD/day) and FR is 4.3k EUR. Both negative or marginal across the week. Recommend fixing targeting / driving spend up first, then add rules once there's ≥ ~3 months at meaningful volume.

What's missing to go from this analysis to deployable rules

Pattern stability over multiple weeks

The DoW and hour-of-day cuts above are 90-day averages. Before committing rules, sanity-check that the patterns hold across the last 3–4 individual weeks (not just one dramatic week distorting the average). Particularly relevant for the IE Saturday spike and DE Saturday loss — both could be sampling artefacts.

Stability check

This is a 90-day average. Before committing rules, sanity-check that the DoW patterns hold over at least the last 3–4 individual weeks (not just one dramatic week distorting the average). Particularly relevant for the IE inversion and DE Sat loss.

Account ↔ Country alignment

AU Tableau spend (4,581) vs AU Meta account spend (12,249 AUD) suggests the AU Meta account is also serving NZ or other-country traffic. Confirm which Meta accounts produce traffic in which countries — rules run on accounts, but performance lives in countries. Mismatch = wasted rule action.
