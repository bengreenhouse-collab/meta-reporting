Meta budget vs APR — does scaling up hurt?

Hypothesis test: when ad-set spend rises week-over-week, does APR per £ fall? Window: 12 weeks (2026-03-02 → 2026-05-24). Source: Tableau APR+ datasource, all Meta channels. Built 2026-05-31.

TL;DR — your theory is supported by the data

Across 776 week-over-week change events at Category × Country × Meta level, there is a clear negative relationship between spend increases and APR margin. The pattern holds in aggregate and in 90% of high-spend categories tested. Estimated APR foregone from over-scaling, just in the categories with the strongest signal:

The aggregate signal: when weekly spend went up 25-50%, margin dropped by a median of 34 percentage points. When spend went up >50%, margin dropped by a median of 35 points and a mean of 41 points. The pattern is monotonic and symmetric — cuts of similar magnitude restored margin by similar amounts.

Recommended actions

  1. Stop reactive scale-up rules. Any automated rule that increases ad-set budget by >25% on positive APR signal is almost certainly destroying APR margin within 1-2 weeks. The data is monotonic: every spend-up bucket from +10% onwards shows margin loss.
  2. Set per-category weekly spend caps near the "best-margin week" level + 30% headroom. The "missed APR" column shows what each category gave up by spending above its efficient frontier. The biggest UK case (Bathroom Installation) has £22k of foregone APR alone over 12 weeks. Capping at ~£3k/week (1.3× best week £2,326) would have recovered most of that.
  3. Rate-limit week-over-week budget increases to +10% max. Every event bucket from +10% onwards shows median margin loss. Below +10%, the median is flat (−0.4pp). Slow scaling preserves margin.
  4. Add a fast-cut-back rule: if a category's weekly margin drops 10pp+ week-over-week, automatic 20% spend cut. The cut buckets all show margin recovery. Reactive defence works; reactive offence doesn't.
  5. Free up budget for under-scaled markets/categories. The "scale up works" list is much smaller and mostly contains categories with negative absolute APR (i.e., they're losing less at higher spend, not making more). Most of the freed budget should not be redeployed into Meta — consider whether it's better spent on Google or other channels with healthier scaling.
  6. Specific top targets for budget cuts (UK): Bathroom Installation (cap £3k/wk), Driveway Installation (currently above efficient frontier — best margin week ran at £4,500/wk but with margin 0%, suggesting the category may have no efficient frontier above £1k/wk), Patio Services (cap £2k/wk), Fence & Gate Installation (cap £1.5k/wk), Artificial Grass Installation (cap £2k/wk).
  7. Specific top targets for budget cuts (US): Wedding Photography (cap £1.7k/wk), Immigration Lawyers (cap £4k/wk), Dog Training (cap £4k/wk), TV Installation & Mounting (cap £500/wk).
